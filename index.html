Responsive Design Strategy

1. Passive Architecture Strategy

  • Building Orientation: Focus on northeast orientation for main spaces, minimizing openings towards the southwest.
  • Overhangs & Shading: Add wide overhangs, pergolas, and a secondary skin to block direct sunlight and rain.
  • Cross-Ventilation: Enhance natural cross-ventilation throughout all indoor and semi-indoor areas.
  • Voids & Atriums: Use internal voids/atriums to facilitate vertical air movement (stack effect).

2. Landscape & Drainage Strategy

  • Permeable Surfaces: Implement porous paving in parking and pedestrian areas to prevent water runoff.
  • Bioswales & Rain Gardens: Create natural channels and catchments for rainwater runoff management.
  • Green Buffer: Use dust and pollutant filtering plants (e.g., bamboo, ficus) on the site perimeter.

3. Material & Construction

  • Resistant Materials: Use heat and humidity resistant materials like exposed brick, local stone, and PU sandwich panel roofs.
  • Ventilated Facades: For west and south-facing areas, use ventilated facades or vertical greenery to reduce heat gain.

4. Comfort & Health

  • Air Quality Measures: Add indoor vegetation & air purifiers. Minimize construction during poor AQI periods.
  • Misting Zones: Use misting or air curtains to improve microclimate comfort in semi-outdoor areas (e.g., event lawn, café).

5. Energy Management

  • Solar Panels: Optimize flat roofs for solar panels to support energy sustainability.
  • Lighting Strategy: Use daylight sensors & LED lamps in showrooms and transition areas to save energy.

6. Zone Connectivity

  • Shaded Circulation: Create semi-open pedestrian paths integrated with the landscape.
  • Transitional Spaces: Add buffer zones (terraces, balconies, verandas) to aid thermal and functional transitions.
